Historic Places In Cape Town

District Six Museum

  • This museam gives a remembrance to the events of the apartheid era
  • The ground floor is covered by a large street map of District Six.
  • Its function as a museum serves as a memorial to the community

Parliament of RSA

  • The building consists of three main sections: the original building,
    completed in 1884, and additions constructed in the 1920s and 1980s.
  • The original parliament building was designed in a Neoclassical style
  • Queen Victoria granted permission for the establishment of a parliament in the Cape Colony in 1853

City Hall

  • The building was designed as the result of a public competition
  • The workmanship and materials are of high quality
  • The tower of the City Hall has a Turret Clock which strikes the hours
